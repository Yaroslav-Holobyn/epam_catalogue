Pride and Prejudice

Jane Austen

The story centers on the Bennet family, particularly the sharp-witted and independent Elizabeth Bennet, who lives in a society where marriage is often treated as a matter of financial necessity rather than personal happiness. Under constant pressure from her mother to secure a suitable match, Elizabeth navigates social expectations, family obligations, and her own strong sense of morality. The arrival of the wealthy and good-natured Mr. Bingley, along with his reserved and proud friend Mr. Darcy, disrupts the calm order of the neighborhood. Through misunderstandings, personal growth, and moments of self-reflection, the novel explores themes of class, pride, prejudice, and the true nature of love.

Classic Romance Social Satire

Moby-Dick

Herman Melville

Narrated by the wandering and reflective sailor Ishmael, this novel follows the perilous journey of the whaling ship Pequod as it sails across the vast and unforgiving oceans. The crew is commanded by the obsessive and enigmatic Captain Ahab, whose sole purpose is to hunt down Moby Dick, the legendary white whale that maimed him years before. What begins as a whaling expedition slowly transforms into a dark meditation on obsession, fate, and the limits of human will. Rich in symbolism and philosophical depth, the novel examines humanity’s struggle against the overwhelming power of nature.

Adventure Epic Philosophical

The Picture of Dorian Gray

Oscar Wilde

Set in the refined yet morally conflicted world of Victorian London, the novel tells the story of Dorian Gray, a young man of extraordinary beauty whose portrait is painted by the artist Basil Hallward. Influenced by the cynical and hedonistic philosophy of Lord Henry Wotton, Dorian becomes obsessed with youth and pleasure. After wishing that the portrait would age in his place, his desire is mysteriously fulfilled. As Dorian’s outward appearance remains untouched by time, the hidden painting becomes a disturbing reflection of his moral decay, raising profound questions about art, beauty, and responsibility.

Gothic Philosophical Psychological

Jane Eyre

Charlotte Brontë

The novel follows the life of Jane Eyre, an orphaned girl who endures a harsh and lonely childhood marked by cruelty and neglect. Through intelligence, moral strength, and quiet determination, Jane grows into an independent young woman and becomes a governess at the mysterious Thornfield Hall. There she forms a deep emotional connection with her employer, the brooding Edward Rochester. However, their relationship is threatened by dark secrets hidden within the estate. The novel explores themes of identity, love, morality, and the struggle for equality in a rigid social order.
